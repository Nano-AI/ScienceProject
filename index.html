Newton's First Law

About the First Law

According to NASA's Website, the first law says that "Every object persists in its state of rest or uniform motion in a straight line unless it is compelled to change that state by forces impressed on it.".
In basic terms, an object will stay at rest or go in one direction unless another force is acted upon it. An example could be a still soccer ball. It's going to stay still unless some other force acts upon it. An example of an external force could be my leg. When I kick it, it's going to change the direction of movement.
